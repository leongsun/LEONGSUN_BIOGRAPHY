A Life in Progress: From Play to Purpose

Childhood Years

1I grew up as a naughty and noisy kid who loved running around. 2I spent most of my time with classmates laughing over simple things. 3Toys were my favorite thing and I played with them for hours. 4I wasn't into gadgets yet because I preferred physical play. 5My childhood felt pure and joyful with lots of carefree moments. 6I loved going out with my family for gala-gala and bonding. 7We enjoyed eating together and trying new food. 8We also watched movies whenever we could. 9Playing at home or outdoors made every day exciting. 10Even though I was shy, I made friends easily at school. 11My classmates became my companions in fun and mischief. 12I looked forward to school like it was another playground. 13My schedule was the usual 6am to 2pm routine. 14After school I kept playing until dinner time. 15My parents made sure we spent time together as a family. 16As an only child I valued connecting with others. 17That's why I was always eager to play and bond with people. 18I slept early, usually around 8pm or 9pm. 19My days were long and full of energy. 20Looking back, childhood was a chill time in my life.

Teenage Years

1As a teenager, life shifted from carefree to demanding. 2Stress started creeping in because decisions mattered more. 3Senior high made me choose a path for my future. 4I had to decide between STEM, HUMSS, or ABM. 5I was always drawn to technology since childhood. 6I liked automation, coding, and how systems worked. 7So choosing STEM felt right for me. 8It was tough at first but my interests guided me. 9Gadgets became a bigger part of my life. 10School work required using technology more often. 11Playing with toys slowly faded away. 12This time became about exploration and curiosity. 13I wanted to learn new things I never tried before. 14I got into sports like soccer. 15I also discovered music through the violin. 16This was when I tried coding for the first time. 17My school schedule grew longer than before. 18I became pickier with friendships. 19Conversations felt more mature and intimidating. 20My teenage years taught me bravery and voice.

College Years

1College hit different with all the new pressures. 2Unlike senior high, this felt like the real deal. 3I worried about professors, classmates, and pressure. 4These thoughts made me anxious during my first weeks. 5My schedule was shocking compared to before. 6College gave more freedom but demanded long hours. 7Some days I left at 7am and got home at 6pm. 8Commuting in Manila traffic was another challenge. 9Rush hour made every trip exhausting. 10By the time I got home I felt drained. 11Classes, projects, and deadlines were constant. 12Managing time became critical. 13College wasn't just studying but balancing everything. 14My social life became more meaningful. 15I learned to be very social with classmates. 16We went out for gala-gala during free time. 17I took my programming hobby seriously. 18I joined hackathons to gain experience. 19I learned independence and responsibility. 20My sleep got worse, sometimes sleeping at 2am because of projects and doom scrolling.
